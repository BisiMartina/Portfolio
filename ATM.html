App ATM Milano

For this University project, we analyzed the functionalities of the ATM app, identified the problems with its use, and developed an interactive prototype, which was tested on real users.

The project was completed in collaboration with three people for the Master’s course Cognitive Ergonomics.

Within the project, we analyzed the functionalities of the app to identify problematic aspects of the user experience. We identified solutions and implemented them in an interactive prototype. Then, we tested the prototype by creating scenarios with user personas to simulate various use cases. Finally, we analyzed the collected data and improved the prototype.

Concept

ATM Milano is an application designed to simplify your travel within the city in an easy and effective manner, optimizing time through complete digitalization of the processes. The ATM app was created to streamline the user experience of the service, offering the ability to purchase tickets, renew subscriptions, calculate routes and schedules, and report any issues on the lines.

This project is based on our personal experience with ATM services and its corresponding application. We frequently use the ATM app to navigate the city; however, its usage is not always as simple and intuitive as it could be.

The aim of this project is to analyze certain functionalities of the application to identify problematic aspects and provide possible solutions to make its use more straightforward.

Problem identification

We have analyzed the application in depth and identified some usability issues.

1. High number of steps to find the schedule of a tram or bus line

2. Too many steps and possible routes to find the metro schedules

3. Inability to use a digital version of one's subscription

Proposed solutions & Prototype

Line stop schedules

Retrieving the stop schedules requires several steps. Starting from the Home Page and using the Lines function, the number of steps is six. Through the Around function, the number of steps is reduced to four, but if the Around function is used to search for stops far from the geolocated point, one would need to enter the name of the stop or address, increasing the number of steps to five.

In our prototype, we decided to reduce the number of steps within the Lines function to three steps to view the schedule of the stop of interest, starting from the Home Page.

Metro stop schedules

The retrieval of metro schedules can be done in the Lines section by clicking on the various metro lines, with the number of steps still being six. Additionally, this is often not the first place one thinks to look for schedules, given the prominence of the metro icons on the Home Page in a section called Metro Status, where one might expect to find information related to the different lines.

We decided to use this section to include additional information related to metro services. Besides the existing feature of viewing metro regularity, we added the option to access the metro network map and schedules through two buttons within the section. to view the schedule of the stop of interest, starting from the Home Page.

Digital Subscription

The application includes a section dedicated to your subscription and ATM services, accessible upon logging into your ATM profile directly from the Home Page. The app allows users to top up their subscription directly within the app and view their cards.

We decided to add the possibility to use one's subscription directly through the app, thereby digitizing it, reducing the number of printed cards by ATM, and lowering the risk of loss.

Additional Changes

We also considered other possible modifications to make the app more intuitive and usable:

  • Adding a header to the Home Page with the text "Where do you want to go?"
  • Removing the Search icon on the Home Page for being a redundant feature
  • Redesigning the side menu
  • Adding the possibility to change lines at stations that intersect two metro lines in the schedules section

User tests

To test the users, we used the Task Analysis technique, as the concept of task is central to evaluating systems. For the testing phase, we created user personas inserted into scenarios with objectives. We identified the actions they would need to perform to achieve their goal, thus obtaining tasks to be carried out.
The tasks are designed based on usage scenarios, and being an application dedicated to transportation, it was necessary to identify routes that users could follow to find the schedules as required.

Tasks

1. Searching for tram line schedules.

2. Searching for metro line schedules.

3. Using the digital subscription.
