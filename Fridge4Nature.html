Fridge4Nature

The aim of the project was to design a website for a product or service aligned with the goals of the 2030 Agenda for Sustainable Development. The project was developed by a team of 6 students for a University Master’s course on visual communication and interface design.

As a team, we designed and developed a responsive website for F4N, an application for Smartphones and Smart Fridges that promotes a sustainable and healthy diet. Within the project, we analyzed competitors, identified the target audience, created a moodboard, selected a palette, defined typography, and designed the logo. We established a design system, created mockups for the website and then we developed it using the Bootstrap framework.

SmartFood

Strenghts

  • Various services including tips for conscious shopping
  • Content organized into subcategories

Weaknesses

  • Page space poorly utilized
  • Very crowded information

Smartfood IEO provides information and tools, based on scientific knowledge, to help people understand the components of daily nutrition, with the aim of promoting health. The main goal of the program is scientific dissemination in the field of nutrition and health: creating and spreading messages endorsed by the scientific community, combating misinformation and information asymmetry, and encouraging conscious food choices to promote health and prevention. We drew inspiration from this site for the use of vibrant colors and the division of content into sections.

SU-EATABLE LIFE

Strenghts

  • Minimalist style
  • Clear and readable writing style for everyone
  • Sustainable recipe book

Weaknesses

  • Extremely disproportionate page margins
  • Text is not centered and too close together

The three-year SU-EATABLE LIFE project aims to demonstrate the potential for reducing CO2 emissions and water consumption through the adoption of healthy and sustainable diets. The project seeks to show that such a diet benefits not only people's health but also the environment, with an estimated saving of about 5,300 tons of CO2 equivalent and about 2 million cubic meters of water over the three years of the project, significantly contributing to the European goal of reducing CO2 emissions by 40% by 2030. We took inspiration from this site for the idea of a goal counter, but modified its function. For SU-EATABLE, it is a counter for future goals, while for us, it is a counter of achieved milestones.

Biorfarm

Strenghts

  • Use of simple icons
  • Clean and essential style
  • Tree adoption service

Weaknesses

  • Low visibility of the shopping cart in the e-commerce section
  • Chat only active during certain hours

Adopt, monitor, enjoy: this is the paradigm of Biorfarm, where individuals and businesses can adopt or gift fruit trees, olive trees, vines, or beehives, monitor their cultivation online, and then receive fresh products delivered to their doorstep, directly from the fields to the table. The project is based on two pillars: local producers and end consumers, with the aim of creating a relationship between them, promoting the quality and traditions of small local farmers with a model that has a high social and environmental impact on the territory. On this site, we found the concept of tree adoption applied, along with a series of illustrations that closely align with what later became our visual identity.

Target

Our target audience is people from 18 to 65 years old:

  • People who are aware of the issues our planet is facing. They already follow a healthy and sustainable diet and contribute to its preservation, and they want to continue doing so in a more interactive way by using the services our application offers.
  • People who are aware of the issues but do not know how to make their diet healthier and more sustainable.
  • People completely unaware of the issues, but by obtaining the correct and interactive information through our application about what our planet is facing, they would follow a healthy and sustainable diet.
