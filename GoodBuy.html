GoodBuy

A project aiming to design the Smartphone application Goodbuy, which allows the sale, the purchase and the exchange of furniture and everyday-use items. It consists of a convivial tool, within the theme of circular economy.

The project has been completed with the collaboration of 5 people for the Master’s course Laboratory of Interaction Design.

Within the project, we tackled the identification of the target and the needs of the users by means of a survey, analysed the competitors, designed the architecture of the application through card sorting, created the logo, the wireframe and an interactive prototype. Finally, we applied heuristic evaluations and user tests.

Concept

Reflecting on the theme for our project, we started from my real-life experience abroad for the Erasmus+ Traineeship program. While abroad, I had to buy some furniture (e.g., lamp, cushions) to decorate my bedroom. However, I then wondered: what would happen to all the items I bought once I returned to Italy? My new friends suggested using dedicated WhatsApp groups to resell my items, but I feared I wouldn't have enough time to sell them before I left.

Given these considerations, we have thought of creating an app that facilitates the exchange, purchase, and sale of household items among people who frequently change their residence (for work, study, or other reasons) and cannot take furniture and/or useful household items with them when they move.

Our application aims to be an example of circular economy based on the reuse of materials and goods that are still in good condition, but also serves as a convivial tool that facilitates exchanges among people who travel and move frequently.

Survey

The first step was to gather demographic data, behaviors, and habits of people regarding the purchase and sale of second-hand items. More specifically, the goal was to identify the ideal target audience and understand if GoodBuy—with its features—could fully meet user needs, thus representing an innovation in the landscape of second-hand buying and selling apps.

Two questionnaires were created: one in Italian and another in English. We chose to create an English version (you can find it here) because we aim to reach an international audience as well. For the English version, we relied on a native speaker for the translation, and a translator and linguistic mediator for the revision.

The questionnaires were created using Google Forms and distributed via WhatsApp, Telegram, and Instagram. The responses received were respectively:

107

The questionnaire was designed to collect information anonymously and confidentially, in compliance with current regulations. During the analysis phase, the collected data was used exclusively for research purposes.

Competitor & SWOT Analysis

We identified two main competitors.

  • Vinted: Website and mobile app dedicated to the purchase, sale, and exchange of new or second-hand items. Originally focused only on selling women's clothing, it later expanded to include children's and men's clothing, accessories like jewelry, bags, books, children's toys, small household items, and pet supplies. Additionally, it allows users to advertise their products to increase the likelihood of sales.
  • Ebay Kleinanzeigen: it is a website and mobile app that allows people to buy and sell second-hand goods between individuals in Germany. Founded in 2009 by eBay, it enables users to search and post free ads for items such as furniture, appliances, clothes, toys, books, and much more. Additionally, the website offers specific sections for job offers, other services, and real estate.

SWOT Analysis

Vinted
Strengths
  • User-friendly: Vinted's app and website are easy to use and provide a simple and intuitive user experience, promoting customer loyalty.
  • High convenience: It offers users an economical and convenient solution for buying and selling used clothes, thanks to low fees and the ability to negotiate directly with sellers.
  • Wide selection of products: With a large user base and the continuous growth of the platform, Vinted offers a wide selection of used fashion products, increasing the chances of finding what you're looking for.
Weaknesses
  • Product quality: Since Vinted is a platform for selling used products, the quality of the items offered can vary significantly and may be a concern for some buyers.
  • Slow assistance: The process of resolving issues between sellers and buyers can be lengthy and frustrating.
  • Counterfeits: Like many online platforms, Vinted might face issues with counterfeit products, which could harm the company's reputation.
Opportunities
  • Verify product quality: With Vinted, it is possible to check the quality of products only after receiving them, whereas with GoodBuy, this can be done before the purchase.
  • Selling large items: Vinted does not offer the ability to sell large items (e.g., a chair).
  • Drop off in affiliated stores: Having a platform more oriented towards the needs of people in transit or those who prefer to avoid contact with strangers.
Threats
  • Expansion: Vinted could decide to expand its offerings to include more household items, establishing itself not only for clothing but also for furniture, appliances, and more.
  • Loyalty: Vinted's community is already very developed, making it difficult to convince users to switch platforms, as they are accustomed to certain features and a specific interface.
  • Fast shipping: Vinted boasts an efficient and fast shipping service.
eBay Kleinanzeigen
Strengths
  • Large user base: It has a large user base of both buyers and sellers, ensuring a wide selection of products for sale.
  • Convenient and economical: The platform is free and easy to use, making it a convenient and attractive option for those looking to sell or buy second-hand products.
  • Reliability: As a subsidiary of eBay, the company enjoys a solid international reputation, which can increase buyers' and sellers' trust in the platform.
Weaknesses
  • Transaction security: Transactions between buyers and sellers can be complicated, especially for high-value products, and there are risks of fraud and scams.
  • Personal safety risks: Personal meetings required for product exchanges can pose personal safety risks.
Opportunities
  • Expanding to the European market: eBay operates only in Germany, providing us with the opportunity to reach a wider audience in other nations.
  • Listing unavailable products: Some listings feature products that are no longer available. GoodBuy provides a system that manages sales.
  • Limited buyer protection: eBay Kleinanzeigen offers limited protection for buyers in case of issues because purchases are managed externally. An internal management system allows for checks and verifications.
Threats
  • Recognized brand: eBay has a recognized brand and a large audience, making it difficult to compete with.
  • Free listings: It offers the possibility of posting free ads, making it accessible to anyone looking to sell or buy products online in Germany.
GoodBuy
Strengths
  • Collection points: The presence of affiliated second-hand shops can offer a practical solution for those who do not want or cannot transport their items to a new location.
  • Connection: The local nature of the app can make it easier to connect with people in the same area and allow for greater trust in the transaction, creating a community of Goodbuyers.
  • Product quality: The ability to inspect the product before purchase in second-hand shops allows users to verify its actual quality before buying.
Weaknesses
  • Shipping: Lack of a shipping service that allows the purchase of goods sold in locations different from where the user is.
  • User base: It might be challenging to attract a sufficient number of users to generate significant transaction volume.
Opportunities
  • Growing market: The market is rapidly expanding as more people frequently move for work or study.
  • Environmental impact: The idea of reusing items could attract consumers concerned about the environmental impact of their purchases.
  • Savings: The application could offer a practical and economical solution for those looking to save money while promoting the circular economy.
Threats
  • Competition: There could be competition from already established similar applications.
  • Resource scarcity: There might be a shortage of useful household items available for exchange, especially in certain geographical areas.
  • Trust: Users might lack trust in the exchange system, especially in the initial phase.

Heuristics Evaluation

Card Sorting

At this stage, we adopted the Card Sorting method, considered useful initially to observe and understand how users categorized the information available to them, and subsequently to confirm or restructure the app's information architecture.

The Card Sorting – created online using the Miro platform – was administered to 6 participants: 3 men and 3 women aged between 23 and 33, representing different target groups (out-of-town student, Erasmus student, business traveler, and regular resident).

Thanks to the selection of a heterogeneous sample of subjects, we were able to obtain a broader and more representative view of the mental models through which users tend to design information.

Mockups & Design System

After reading and analyzing the feedback received from the experts, we proceeded to create the mockups, incorporating the following changes:

  • Introduction of the FAQ in the settings
  • Replacement of the written categories on the Home screen with the same icons used in the sales section.

Prototype & User Tests

After creating the mock-ups, we conducted a new evaluation to test their effectiveness through a task analysis. We administered 11 tasks to users to assess the impact of the changes made to the prototype. The tests were conducted both in-person and remotely using the Figma software, and critical dialogue was encouraged (think-aloud technique). We selected 8 participants who could belong to the user base of our application.

Tasks:

  1. Open the Goodbuy application and view the products in the “Sport” category.
  2. Return to the home screen and view the details of the “Jumbo Resale” store.
  3. Select the store located on Metropolitan Avenue from the map and access its details.
  4. Identify the “Unread” notifications.
  5. View your “Unread” messages.
  1. Send a message to “Noah Cooper,” a member of your community and the seller of the fridge.
  2. Indicate how to edit your personal information.
  3. View your balance and your favorite products.
  4. Log out.
  5. Log in, then purchase the refrigerator and pay with your credit card.
  6. List your towels for sale.

Results

  • Difficulty recognizing the unread message.
  • Difficulty recognizing the new notification.
  • Misinterpretation of the categories.
  • Difficulty locating the newly uploaded product within their profile.
  • Inability to identify community members solely through the profile picture.
